# Sugarloaf Stakeholder Review Checklist

This checklist is for Keith and AM to review before the next round of site changes. The current site is a functional draft with safe polish applied, but several content, trust, photo, donation, and authenticity decisions should come from the people closest to Sugarloaf.

Use this as a page-by-page review guide. The goal is not to rewrite everything in one sitting. The goal is to identify which parts are correct, which parts need more Sugarloaf-specific detail, and which parts should wait until the organization is ready to publish them.

## Review Priorities

- [ ] Confirm the official organization name everywhere: currently `Sugarloaf Mountain Ranch, inc.` in most locations and `Sugarloaf Mountain Ranch Inc.` on the check donation line.
- [ ] Confirm preferred capitalization and punctuation for `inc.`, `Inc.`, `501c3`, `501(c)(3)`, `Non-Profit`, and `Farm Animal Rescue and Sanctuary`.
- [ ] Confirm whether the main audience is donors, volunteers, rescue partners, local community members, prospective animal owners, press/media, or all of the above.
- [ ] Confirm whether the website should primarily ask people to donate, learn the story, contact AM, follow Facebook, volunteer, sponsor an animal, or visit/coordinate with the ranch.
- [ ] Confirm which pieces of the current site are publish-ready versus placeholders.
- [ ] Confirm whether the site should sound more personal and founder-led, more formal nonprofit, more urgent fundraising, or more educational.

## Suggested Review Flow

1. Review Global Site Review first so naming, tone, and primary CTA are locked.
2. Review Home, About, Donors, and Contact next because those affect trust and conversion most.
3. Review Pictures once approved photos and captions are available.
4. Review News last, after deciding whether it is press-only or current updates.
5. Keep a separate list of exact replacement copy rather than editing live during discussion.

## Developer Notes

- Most current text lives in `src/lib/siteContent.ts`.
- Page-level headings and section copy also appear in `src/pages`.
- Current site behavior is intentionally simple: no backend is required for donations or contact.
- The contact form opens the user's email client instead of sending through a server.
- Donation buttons currently send users to PayPal.
- Any deeper feature work, such as animal profiles, sponsorships, volunteer forms, or CMS editing, should wait until the above decisions are reviewed.
